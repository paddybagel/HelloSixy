FAQs

Are there discounts for Students?

Yes, we do offer 25% discount to students. Please check the student box and use your student email address when on the file upload page.

How are costs calculated?

We calculate the amount of material used during printing. For both General Purpose Plastics (FDM) & High Detail Resin (SLA) we charge based on the volume of the object. FDM - Printed with a lower infill to save weight, time and material. You will only be charged for the actual amount of material used when printing on these type of printers. We use a default infill of 20% for our initial quote however different settings can be selected once your order has been requested. It is possible to print parts at higher infills but this would incur extra cost. SLA - We charge based on the volume of the object but most SLA prints require support material therefore the cost of that will be calculated after the order request.

Are there size limitations?

Our build volumes are: For General Purpose Plastics (FDM): 285L x 153W x 155H (mm) For High Detailed Resin (SLA): 145L x 145W x 175H (mm) Objects that exceed the maximum build volume can often be sliced into parts and bonded together after printing.

What is support material?

Dependant on the design, both General Purpose Plastics (FDM) and High Detailed Resin (SLA) require support material. These supports are printed at the same time as the part and are positioned as to be easily removed from the model as possible. Please see guide for removing support marks. High Detail Resin (SLA) https://formlabs.com/support/finishing/removing-support-marks/ General Purpose Plastics (FDM) https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GU2i-s--eLc

Can I cancel once I have placed my order?

We start production once payment has gone through, after this time if we have started to produce the model or we have started repairing your mesh then cancellation may incur a charge. If we have not carried out any processing then cancellation may be possible, please email hub@e8make.com as soon as possible if you need to cancel.

Which file types can I upload?

You can upload STL or OBJ files, the standard files used in the 3D printing industry. Note: As STL files don't contain information on what measurement unit was used to create the model, it is important that you know which measurement unit your 3D program uses in mm, cm or inches, before you export it. Once you’re done with the export, you can upload a 3D design for printing and specify the measurement units in mm, cm or inches within the file uploader.

Can you repair files?

Yes we do offer a file fixing service. We will tell you if your file is broken and how much it will cost to fix it. If fixing time is under 10 minutes no cost will incur.

I don't have a 3D digital model, can you still give me a price?

We offer a full CAD service and can create files from descriptions or drawings. Please send us an email to hub@e8make.com with your hopes, dreams and desires and we will get back to you with a quote.

Can I 3D print proprietary, confidential designs under NDA?

Yes, you can. We do not 3D print copies of customer’s designs unless these have been requested by the customer, and we will sign an NDA (non-disclosure agreement) if required. For signing an NDA, please email us at hub@e8make.com describe your project and the NDA requirement before sending details of your enquiry.

What material should I pick for my project?

You can learn about our 3D printing materials and compare them here As a quick rule of thumb: - Basic prototypes without many complex shapes/overhang: General Purpose Plastics - Small intricate parts with a lot of details: Standard High Detail Resin - Strong functional parts: Tough High Detail Resin, Flexible High Detail Resin, General Purpose Plastics (100% Infill) - Parts for casting: Castable High Detail Resin If you are unsure of what material you want to use, we can take a look at your model and give you quotes from multiple materials.

Is my 3D design suitable for printing?

When you upload your 3D design for printing, we check each file for holes (non-manifold geometry) before production. This initial check is to ensure that your model is a so-called solid mesh – a model where all the edges of the polygons that build up the model are connected to one-another (manifold geometry). We also look out for issues with the design and will get back to you if any amendments are necessary. Nevertheless, we highly recommend ensuring that your file meets the basic design requirements for 3D printing beforehand. If you’re unsure, visit our guide for detailed information.

How can I get an invoice for my order?

Once you send the files through we will generate an invoice with a payment gateway to send to you.

Do you have a minimum charge?

We have a minimum part charge which may apply to small parts on smaller orders. The minimum part charge is to cover the cleaning process as it’s very time consuming to hand clean very small things.

Are there discounts for batch production runs?

We offer discount on orders that have large quantities of the same part. Ask us to give you a quantity pricing breakdown on the file upload page.
